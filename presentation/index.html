04
📊
Profit is Not Normal
Why standard statistics can mislead
Shapiro-Wilk test: W = 0.663, p < 0.001 — Profit distribution is highly skewed.

The profit distribution has heavy tails with extreme negative outliers (deep loss transactions). This means the arithmetic mean is a misleading central tendency measure. The median profit of $9.24 is far more representative than the mean. Non-parametric tests should be used when analyzing profit.

Shapiro-Wilk Skewed Data Normality Test

YoY sales grew by ~20% annually, but profit growth lags behind significantly.

Monthly sales show a consistent upward trend with seasonal peaks in Q4. However, profit months show high volatility — indicating that revenue growth is not always translating to profitability. The November–December discount festivals spike sales but crash margins.

Hypothesis Testing

Statistical Validation Results

Rigorous hypothesis tests applied at α = 0.05 significance level

Test 1 — Shapiro-Wilk
H₀: Profit is normally distributed
H₁: Profit is NOT normally distributed
❌ Reject H₀
p < 0.001 | W = 0.663
Profit data is heavily skewed with fat tails. The null hypothesis is decisively rejected. This validates using non-parametric methods for profit analysis.
Test 2 — Independent T-Test
H₀: Consumer = Corporate profit margins
H₁: Margins differ significantly
❌ Reject H₀
p < 0.05 | See hypothesis_test_results.csv
Consumer and Corporate segments have statistically different average profit margins. Corporate customers tend to receive negotiated pricing affecting margins differently.
Test 3 — One-Way ANOVA
H₀: Sales are equal across all regions
H₁: At least one region differs
❌ Reject H₀
p < 0.05 | F-stat from output CSV
Significant sales differences exist across the four regions. West consistently outperforms, while Central underperforms — indicating market or operational disparities that need attention.
Test 4 — Chi-Square
H₀: Ship Mode is independent of Region
H₁: Ship Mode depends on Region
❌ Reject H₀
p < 0.05 | χ² — See output CSV
Customer shipping preferences are NOT independent of region. This suggests regional supply-chain dynamics, customer behavior, or logistics availability drives the choice of ship mode.

Actionable Insights

Data-Driven Recommendations

Statistically-backed business recommendations for the Superstore

🚫
Cap Discounts at 30%
Statistical evidence shows products discounted above 30% consistently yield negative or near-zero profit. Implement a discount ceiling policy and require senior approval for discounts exceeding 25%.
🗺️
Invest in West, Fix Central
ANOVA confirms West region outperforms significantly. Central region needs a dedicated task force to investigate root causes (logistics costs, product mix, pricing) for below-average performance.
💡
Prioritize Technology Sales
Technology generates ~50% of profits at only ~36% of revenue. Expand the technology product portfolio, train sales teams on high-margin tech products, and promote them in all regions.
🪑
Restructure Furniture Category
Tables and Bookcases consistently lose money. Consider price increases, cost reduction through better suppliers, or phasing out loss-making SKUs. Conduct SKU-level profitability audit for Furniture.
📅
Manage Seasonal Discounting
Q4 shows sales spikes but profit crashes due to holiday discounting. Plan smarter promotions: target high-margin products during peaks, limit deep discounts on already-popular items.
📦
Optimize Shipping by Region
Chi-square test shows shipping mode is linked to region (p < 0.05). Analyze shipping cost vs delivery time trade-offs per region and negotiate better rates for high-volume Central region routes.
